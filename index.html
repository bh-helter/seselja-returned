Election 2019: Zed Seselja returned to the upper house

October 30, 2019

Liberal Zed Seselja is scheduled to return to the Senate amid aggressive campaigns against him. Mr. Seselja’s seat had been the target by few such as the Greens, independent Anthony Pesec and the union movement, but no faction succeeded to get enough votes away from the assistant treasurer.

“I think it’s also a bit of a rejection of the politics of personal denigration, we didn’t respond to those we didn’t get into a personality contest with Labor and the Greens and the unions. We did talk about issues that I think many Canberrans care about so I was most pleased about that,” he said.

According to Senator Seselja, he was not troubled by personal campaigns, and the result was an indication that such campaigns had not been successful.

“I know the volunteers when they see those sorts of attacks and family see those kinds of attacks they don’t like it, but obviously the most important thing is that we just respond strongly and spoke about policy issues. To win it is a great relief obviously.”

Senator Seselja said that he was looking forward to going back to the Senate, possibly in government.

“When I woke up this morning I thought, I was hopeful of holding the Senate seat, but obviously the polls were showing us losing and so, it’s amazing to see the way the country has responded to our message and the way that they’ve responded to our prime minister and our team and the policies we’ve put forward.”

Apart from him, his long-standing rival Katy Gallagher also returned to the Senate a year and 10 days after the High Court said that she was ineligible to sit in Parliament.

“A year ago, I didn’t think I wanted to go back into politics, I was pretty shattered by the whole experience and I had a few weeks to think it over and when I said I would renominate I was still in two minds,” she said.
